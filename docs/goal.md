**Temporal Representation and Storage Valuation in Energy System Models: A Comparative Analysis of OSeMOSYS and PyPSA**

Capacity expansion models inform multi-billion dollar infrastructure decisions for offshore renewable energy systems. Uncertainty in the recommendations made by these models is routinely explored through sensitivity analyses and scenario methods. However, model uncertainty, the recognition that multiple valid framings of the same problem exist, receives comparatively little attention. This study proposes a methodology for empirically quantifying model-attributable uncertainty and distinguishing robust insights from modelling artifacts, with a particular focus on offshore wind and storage planning.

We develop an open-source Python package that enables systematic comparison of OSeMOSYS and PyPSA, two widely used capacity expansion frameworks with distinct design philosophies. OSeMOSYS represents energy systems as commodity-flows. Our translation framework distinguishes implementation from structural disagreement. Differences in implicit assumptions that can be harmonized through parameter mappings and pre- or post-processing adjustments. However, structural disagreement is where models represent genuinely different but plausible problem framings. We apply this framework to a stylized North Sea offshore energy hub designed to isolate and quantify sources of model divergence relevant to investment decisions. The case study comprises an offshore wind farm with a representative capacity factor profile exhibiting characteristic North Sea variability, battery storage with configurable duration and efficiency parameters, a fixed-capacity grid connection to shore, and optional gas backup generation. Our simulated onshore demand follows a realistic profile with seasonal and diurnal variation.

We construct a progression of scenarios to systematically probe where model agreement holds and where it breaks down. A wind-only scenario tests how temporal aggregation affects curtailment estimates. Adding short-duration battery storage tests whether the models recommend similar storage capacity when intra-day arbitrage value depends on preserving hourly price spreads versus aggregating to representative timeslices. A long-duration storage variant (hydrogen or compressed air) examines whether the hierarchical seasonal structure in OSeMOSYS adequately captures inter-seasonal shifting despite losing intra-seasonal dynamics.
